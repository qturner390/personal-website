Linear Regression Insights

Metrics:

  • \(R^2\) (0.9871): This means that 98.7% of the variance in gas prices can be directly explained by using changes in crude oil price, unemployment rate, and production. This means the model is highly predictive.
  • MSE (0.0103) and MAE (0.0810):The model's predictions are off by 8.1 cents on average.
  • MAPE (3.6655%):Predictions deviate from the real values by less than 4% on average.

Individual Feature Contributions:

  • Crude Oil Price (coef: 1.2566, p-value: ~0)

    • This is the most predictive feature of the model and the p-value suggests that this relationship is almost certainly not due to random chance. This makes sense since crude oil prices have a direct relationship with gas prices.
    • This feature also has a strong positive relationship with gas prices. For every dollar increase in crude oil price, retail gas prices rise by roughly $1.25.

    • This means that retail gas prices raise by about 5 thousanths of a cent for every thousand gallons produced.
